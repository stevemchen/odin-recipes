French Fries

Description

French fries are potatoes cut up, dried, and then deep-fried in oil. This is typically done in a deep fryer. They can also be oven-baked or air-fried, but are most commonly served with traditional deep frying.

French fries are often made with russet potatoes. There are many variations on the classic dish, such as steak fries and curly fries, but most of these vary only slightly from this delicious treat.

Ingredients

  • Large potato or potatoes, commonly russets
  • Oil for frying
  • Salt for taste

Steps

Steps are taken from Chef John's French Fries on allrecipes.

  1. Cut potato into roughly equal strips.
  2. Soak strips in water for roughly 30 minutes.
  3. Lay out potatoes and pat with paper towels until dry.
  4. Heat your oil in a large pot to 275°F.
  5. Add the potatoes to the oil and fry for about 5 minutes with occasional stirring and flipping.
  6. Transfer fries to paper-towels and let cool completely.
  7. Heat your oil again, but to 350°F.
  8. Re-add your fries and fry a second time, approximately 5 to 6 minutes or until golden brown.
  9. Transfer fries again to paper-towels and lightly blot with paper towel.
  10. Add salt to taste and eat!
